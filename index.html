Getting to Jamaica

Getting to Jamaica is pretty simple, just like any other vaction. On average, fares to fly to Jamaica were around $248-$280. But, depending on what month, prices could be either very expensive or very cheap. For example, the most expensive month tends to be either January, November, and December, most likely because of the cold. The cheapest month tends to be February.

Now, of course, all the prices can change depending on multiple factors, like where you are reletive to Jamaica, taxi services, which airline you are flying, and more. If you want to learn more about getting to Jamaica from where you are, I would go to the website "kayak.com", which tells you everything that you would need to know about getting to Jamaica!

General Info

Jamacia is a mountainious island, located in the Caribbean Sea. It is part of the chain of Caribbean islands called the Greater Antilles, and Cube, Hispaniola, and Puerto Rico are all part of this chain, along with Jamaica as well. If you ever have been on a Cruise, you most likely have been in Jamaica at least once.

As of right now, 5/10/23, the population in 2023 is 2,825,544. Compared to last year, the growth rate is -0.06, the population going down 1,833 citizens. Here is a table listing out the population from 2015 to 2023.

As we can see from this table, the population was on a fairly steady rise, but in 2022, the population started dropping. Thankfully, the decay rate is small, but it does concern people what the future will hold as Jamaica has not ever been in a decline of population since 1950. The U.N. even predicts that by 2100, Jamaica will only have 897,000 citizens.

Things to know before leaving

Firstly, you'll need to understand that different parts of Jamaica are for different travelers. For example, if you like relaxing at beaches and having fun doing water sports, Negril and the north coast of Jamaica would work best for you! What to surround yourself with amazing dining scenes, close to the buzzy nightlife and the awesome natural attractions? Than Montego Bay and Ocho Rios would be lovely! So, before leaving, remember what you want to do in Jamaica, and then plan for which part of the island you want to stay at!

Secondly, the citizens! If you have ever been to outside countries, you most likely have tried to talk to the locals, but then it fell apart right after. But, in Jamaica, feel free to talk to the locals! Of course, some Jamaicans will try to start a conservation so that you can buy what they are selling, but you don't need to worry, as that is rare! Most Jamaicans really just want to know about you and your life, and they find just ingoring them really rude. You'll find yourself greeting everyone! And, remember, always treat the elders with extra resepct.

Finally, just to keep you safe, you should never drink any tap water, no matter what part of the island you are located at. Just like most countries you travel to, you aren't used to the water there, so your stomach won't be able to handle the bactieria in the water and you will, sadly, have an unenjoyable vaction to Jamaica.
