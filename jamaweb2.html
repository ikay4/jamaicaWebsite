History

Introduction

For years now, Jamaica and its citizens have been trying to fight for both their rights and freedoms for now years. Their countrie's past has been the backbone of all citizens motivation to keep going strong, and this part of the Website will go over the important events of Jamaican History so that you can connect with the Jamaicans in a deeper way by understanding what the had to go through to get where they are today.

All sources/citations will be linked in the ABOUT tab.

The Beginning

The first ever people to live in Jamaica was the Tanino Indians, who were around since 600 AD. They were stone-age people, who migerated to Jamaica from present South America. They lived in Jamaica peacefully, until the Spanish found their land, wiping the Tanino Indians due to the exploitation of the Spanish, along with being unable to resist European diseases and most of them starving to death. Some of the Tanino even killed themselves, just so they couldn't become a slave for the Spanish. In the end, all the Tanino Indians were either dead or had become slaves, ending with all the Tanino Indians being killed off.

This now brings us to when the Spanish settled Jamaica after defeating the Tanino Indians. The infamous Christopher Columbus landed in Jamaica in 1494, and after defeating the Tanino Indians, the colonized the land for themselves. Soon after they colonized the land, they built the first ever city in Jamacia, called Sevilla Nueva. At first, Sevilla Nueva was located in Saint Ann's Ba, but was later moved to the capital of Jamaica, called Spanish Town. Now, of course, Jamaica was never really the busy due to the fact that no gold was found on the island, and during that time, Jamaica mainly served as a food supply for ships that were sailing between the Americas and Eruope.

The British Period

In 1655, the British then arrived in Jamaica and captured it from the Spanish, taking over Jamaica for more than 300 years. Britain capture Jamaica from the Spanish due to it being a strategic location, in the centre of the Caribbean, to challenge the Spanish and interruping their trade. Many of the settlers was just land-owners, but others were pirates, who attacked the Spanish Ships which carried gold and silver, the Priates HQ at Port Royal became rich with all the stolen goods.

Later on, Port Royal would become destoryed with earthquakes and hurricanes, but by then the English and Spanish already signed a peace treaty, with pirates not being needed anymore. Jamaica just become a trading port with the exportation of sugar, cocoa, and more. Because of the destoryed Royal Port, it led to the rise of the nearby fishing village, Kingston , becoming the capital of Jamaica in 1872.

Resistance to Slavery

Started by the Spanish and intensivied under British ruling, slavery in Jamaica was a problem for a long time. As production of goods like sugar increased, the demand for slaves went up as well. Even though back in the 18th century, Jamaica was the most valueable colonies, the conditions slaves had to go through was the hellish, many familes being sperated, tourtured, and killed off.

Later on though, the slave trade was abolished in the yeat 1807. But by then, ~2,000,000 slaves were traded to Jamaica, many of those slaves dieing on the ships. But, slavery was gone for good, just the trade. It took citizens another 31 years to finally get rid of slavery for good. In total, it took citizens around 250 years of rebellion to get rid of slavery forever.

However, the end of slavery brought the collapse of plantation systems as the former slaves refused to work for their masters anymore. This, along with other factors, caused a crisis in 1865, calling for a change in social and economic patterns quickly. This caused the Morant Bay Rebellion, which ended up with the leaders of Jamacia being hung. From now on, it was up to the British to handle Jamaica, leading to the British Governor introducing a House of Assembly, which was put into place by the British Parliament in 1866.

Independence

Now, going forward many years, the dissatisfaction with the crown colony system, which was made worse by the suffering because of worldwide econmic depression, caused widespread rioting in Jamaica. These riots resulted in the first lasting labour unions, as well as political parties which were linked to the labour unions. It was apparent that the Jamaicans had a growing demand for self-determination.

Political Agaitations and Trade union leaders led to the granting of a new constitution, introduced in 1944, which called for a 2 party H.o.R, as well as Ministers and adult suffrage. The constitution was also advanced multiple times in 1953 and 1957, with Jamaica obtaining full internal self-government in 1959.

Jamaica then became of a founding memeber of teh Deferation of the West Indies, seceding from it in 1961 after some troubles. Then, finally, after over 300 years of many Jamaicans fighting, rioting, and being killed, Jamaica became an Independence nation in 1962, having full dominion status with the commonwealth!
